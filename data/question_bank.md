#Level 1 — Basic Story Understanding
##Item: Main-character
context:
Milo is a small helper robot who lives in a futuristic city.
One morning, Milo wakes up in a repair shop with no memory of how he got there.
He only remembers one thing: he must find someone named Lina.
Throughout the story, Milo searches the city, asks for help, and slowly learns about his past.

objective:
Verify that the user understands who the main character is and what his basic goal is.



##Item: Setting-basic
context:
The story takes place in a futuristic city filled with tall glass buildings, flying buses, robot assistants, and glowing street signs.
The city is busy, noisy, and full of advanced technology.
Milo travels through different parts of the city, including a repair shop, a market, a train station, and an old observatory.
The setting helps make Milo’s journey feel exciting and unfamiliar.

objective:
Check whether the user understands where the story takes place.



##Item: Memory-loss
context:
At the beginning of the story, Milo cannot remember most of his past.
He does not know why he was in the repair shop or what happened before he woke up.
His only clue is the name Lina, which is saved inside his damaged memory chip.
This mystery pushes Milo to begin his journey.

objective:
Ensure the user understands the problem that starts the story.



##Item: Lina-basic
context:
Lina is the person Milo is trying to find.
At first, Milo does not remember who Lina is.
Later, he learns that Lina is the young inventor who built him and treated him like a friend, not just a machine.
Finding Lina becomes important because she is connected to Milo’s identity and memories.

objective:
Confirm that the user understands who Lina is in relation to Milo.



#Level 2 — Story Reasoning and Interpretation
##Item: Milo-motivation
context:
Milo continues searching for Lina even when the city becomes dangerous and confusing.
He faces broken streets, suspicious security drones, and people who do not want to help him.
Even though he is scared, he keeps going because Lina’s name is the only meaningful memory he has.
His search is not only about finding another person, but also about understanding who he is.

objective:
Check whether the user can explain why Milo keeps searching for Lina.



##Item: Trusting-strangers
context:
During his journey, Milo meets an old street vendor who offers to help him find the observatory.
At first, Milo is unsure whether to trust the vendor because he has already been misled by another robot earlier in the story.
However, the vendor gives Milo useful information without asking for anything in return.
This moment shows Milo slowly learning how to judge who can be trusted.

objective:
Verify that the user can reason about why trust is difficult for Milo.



##Item: Observatory-meaning
context:
The old observatory is where Lina and Milo used to watch the stars together.
When Milo reaches the observatory, some of his memories return.
He remembers Lina teaching him that machines can still make choices, even if they are programmed.
The observatory is important because it connects Milo’s lost memories with the emotional heart of the story.

objective:
Check whether the user understands the deeper meaning of the observatory.



##Item: Ending-choice
context:
At the end of the story, Milo finds Lina, but he also discovers that restoring all of his memories could erase the new experiences he gained during his journey.
Lina tells him the choice is his.
Milo decides to keep both the damaged memories and the new ones, even though he will never remember everything perfectly.
This ending shows that identity is shaped by both the past and the choices made in the present.

objective:
Ensure the user can interpret Milo’s final decision and what it says about identity.
